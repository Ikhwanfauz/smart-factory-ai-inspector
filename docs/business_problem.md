\# Business Problem



Manufacturing companies often rely on manual visual inspection to detect product defects. Manual inspection can be slow, inconsistent, tiring for operators, and difficult to scale when production volume increases.



This project simulates an AI-assisted quality-control system that helps detect surface defects from product images and supports inspection traceability through OCR, database logging, and dashboard analytics.



\## Problem Statement



Manual inspection may lead to:



\- inconsistent defect detection

\- missed defects due to human fatigue

\- slow inspection process

\- limited inspection history

\- difficulty tracking defect trends over time



\## Proposed Solution



The Smart Factory AI Inspector uses computer vision to detect surface defects, OCR to extract product label or serial number information, and a dashboard to monitor inspection results.



The goal is not to replace human inspectors completely, but to support them with faster and more consistent AI-assisted inspection.



\## Target Users



\- Quality-control engineers

\- Manufacturing technicians

\- Automation engineers

\- Production supervisors

\- AI/ML engineers working on industrial inspection systems



\## Expected Benefits



\- Faster inspection process

\- More consistent defect classification

\- Digital inspection history

\- Easier defect trend analysis

\- Better support for manufacturing decision-making
